The Fibonacci sequence is defined by the recurrence relation:

<center>F<sub>n</sub> = F<sub>n−1</sub> + F<sub>n−2</sub>, where F<sub>1</sub> = 1 and F<sub>2</sub> = 1.</center>

Hence the first 12 terms will be:

<center>
F<sub>1</sub> = 1
F<sub>2</sub> = 1
F<sub>3</sub> = 2
F<sub>4</sub> = 3
F<sub>5</sub> = 5
F<sub>6</sub> = 8
F<sub>7</sub> = 13
F<sub>8</sub> = 21
F<sub>9</sub> = 34
F<sub>10</sub> = 55
F<sub>11</sub> = 89
F<sub>12</sub> = 144
</center>

The 12th term, F<sub>12</sub>, is the first term to contain three digits.
